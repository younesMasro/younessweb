If you search for a restaurant, plumber, or agency "near me" on your phone, what shows up first — even before the regular results — is the **Google Maps block with local businesses**. That's exactly what Google Business Profile (formerly Google My Business) lets you get, for free.

It's one of the most powerful and most under-used SEO levers among Moroccan businesses.

## What is Google Business Profile?

Google Business Profile is a **free** Google tool that lets you create an official listing for your business, shown in **Google Maps**, in the **local pack** of Google search results, and in searches like "[your trade] in [your city]" or "[your trade] near me".

## Why it's essential for a Moroccan business

- **Immediate visibility**, without waiting for classic SEO results (which take several months).
- **Free**, unlike paid advertising.
- **Reinforced trust** thanks to customer reviews visible directly in search results.
- **Practical information** (hours, phone, directions) directly accessible to your customers, without even visiting your website.

## How to create your Google Business Profile listing

**Step 1: Create the account.** Go to [google.com/business](https://www.google.com/business/) and create your listing with your business's exact name.

**Step 2: Choose the right category.** The main category must precisely match your activity ("Website design agency", "Moroccan restaurant", "Hair salon", etc.). It's one of the most important factors for showing up in the right searches.

**Step 3: Verify your business.** Google requires verification (usually by postcard mailed to your address, sometimes by phone/email depending on the case). This step is essential for your listing to become publicly visible.

**Step 4: Fill in every field.** Don't leave anything blank: **exact address** and service area if you travel to customers; precise **opening hours** (including holidays); **phone number** and WhatsApp link if applicable; **website** (direct link to your homepage or a dedicated page); **business description**, naturally including your main keywords; **quality photos**: storefront, interior, team, products, or work samples.

## How to optimize your listing for better ranking

**Actively collect customer reviews.** The number and quality of your Google reviews is one of the most important local ranking factors. Systematically ask happy customers to leave a review, and **respond to every review**, positive or negative — it shows Google (and future customers) that your business is active and attentive.

**Post regularly.** Google Business Profile lets you publish **posts** (offers, news, new products) directly on your listing, like a social network. An active listing is treated better by the algorithm than an abandoned one.

**Add photos regularly.** Listings with recent, numerous photos statistically generate more clicks and calls than those with few or no visuals.

**Answer questions quickly.** The "Questions and Answers" section of your listing is publicly visible — monitor it and respond quickly, since other users can also answer in your place, sometimes with incorrect information.

## Common mistakes to avoid

- **Creating multiple listings** for the same business (Google may suspend duplicates).
- **Using too generic a category** ("Business" instead of your precise activity).
- **Leaving outdated hours**, especially around holidays or Ramadan.
- **Ignoring negative reviews** instead of responding professionally.
- **Never posting new photos or updates** after the initial setup.

## The link between Google Business Profile and your website

An optimized Google Business Profile naturally redirects some of its traffic to your website — but the reverse is also true: a well-ranked website reinforces your local listing's credibility in Google's eyes. The two work in synergy, not as a replacement for one another.

## In summary

Google Business Profile is probably the most under-used free tool among Moroccan businesses, even though it enables near-instant visibility in high-intent local searches ("near me", "[trade] in [city]"). Combined with a professional website and regular SEO content, it's one of the pillars of a complete local visibility strategy.
