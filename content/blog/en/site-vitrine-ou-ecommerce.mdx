It's one of the first decisions to make before even contacting a provider: do I need a **showcase site** or an **online store (e-commerce)**? Many Moroccan entrepreneurs hesitate, and the wrong choice can either cost more than necessary or leave you with a site that doesn't match your real need.

## What is a showcase site?

A showcase site presents your business, services, or products **without allowing direct online purchase**. Visitors discover your business, contact you, request a quote, or order by phone/WhatsApp.

**Ideal for**: service providers (consultants, craftspeople, professionals), restaurants and salons that want to present their menu without online sales, B2B businesses where selling happens through custom quotes, physical shops that want to strengthen their online credibility without managing delivery logistics.

## What is an online store (e-commerce)?

An online store lets the customer **choose, order, and pay directly on the site**, with a product catalog, cart, and full checkout process.

**Ideal for**: merchants selling physical products (clothing, cosmetics, crafts, food), brands wanting to expand their sales area beyond their city or neighborhood, businesses wanting to automate order-taking without manual handling by phone.

## The concrete differences

| Criteria | Showcase site | Online store |
|---|---|---|
| Main goal | Inform and generate leads | Sell directly online |
| Technical complexity | Simple to moderate | Moderate to high |
| Average price in Morocco | 2,500 – 7,000 MAD | 5,000 – 12,000+ MAD |
| Time to launch | 1-2 weeks | 3-6 weeks |
| Post-launch management | Occasional content updates | Stock, orders, deliveries |
| Online payment | Not needed | Essential (often + cash on delivery) |

## How to know which one to choose

**Do you sell identifiable, interchangeable products (size, color, reference)?** If yes, an online store makes sense — the customer can choose alone without prior discussion.

**Does your sale require a personalized exchange (quote, measurements, advice)?** If yes, a showcase site with an effective contact form is largely enough — and costs less.

**Can you handle online orders (stock, delivery, customer service)?** A poorly managed online store after launch (unprocessed orders, outdated stock) hurts your image more than a well-maintained simple showcase site.

**Does your current budget allow investing in a full e-commerce setup?** If budget is tight, it's entirely viable to start with a solid showcase site, then evolve toward e-commerce once the business stabilizes.

## A third option: showcase site with simplified ordering

Many Moroccan merchants opt for a very effective middle ground: a showcase site presenting the product catalog, with an **"Order on WhatsApp"** button for each product, rather than a full e-commerce cart.

**Advantages**: cost close to a classic showcase site, no technical online payment management, direct conversation with the customer, which builds a lot of trust in the Moroccan market.

**Limits**: less scalable if order volume grows significantly, no automatic stock management.

This is often the **best starting point** for a merchant wanting to test online selling before investing in a full store.

## Can you move from a showcase site to full e-commerce later?

Yes, absolutely — and it's actually a very common path. If your showcase site is built on a solid foundation (WordPress in particular, see our [WordPress vs Webflow comparison](/blog/wordpress-ou-webflow)), adding an e-commerce module (WooCommerce) later is entirely possible without starting from scratch.

## In summary

| Your situation | Recommended solution |
|---|---|
| Quote-based sales, service, personalized advice | Showcase site |
| Standardized products to sell directly | Online store |
| Limited budget, want to test online selling | Showcase site + WhatsApp ordering |
| E-commerce already validated, need to scale | Full online store |

The right choice depends entirely on your business and operational capacity — not on a trend. A well-thought-out showcase site can generate as much value as a poorly exploited online store.
