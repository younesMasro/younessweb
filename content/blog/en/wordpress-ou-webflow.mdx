When it's time to choose the technology behind your website, two names come up often: **WordPress** and **Webflow**. Both let you build professional sites, but they serve very different needs — especially in the Moroccan context, where cost, autonomy, and local support matter enormously.

Here's an honest comparison, no marketing spin, to help you choose.

## WordPress in brief

WordPress powers over 40% of websites worldwide. It's an open-source content management system (CMS), extendable through thousands of plugins, letting you build anything from a simple showcase site to a full e-commerce store (via WooCommerce).

**Strengths for Morocco**: a very large ecosystem of Moroccan developers and agencies — finding local support is easy. Lower running costs (affordable shared hosting, no mandatory subscription to a foreign platform). Extendable almost infinitely: e-commerce, booking, multilingual, memberships. Excellent SEO potential when properly configured.

**Weaknesses**: requires regular maintenance (security updates, plugins). Quality varies a lot by developer — a poorly built WordPress site can be slow or vulnerable.

## Webflow in brief

Webflow is a visual design tool that generates clean code without requiring you to program. It's popular with designers for the creative freedom it offers.

**Strengths**: very polished, flexible design without relying on pre-built themes. Hosting and security managed directly by Webflow (less technical maintenance). Powerful visual editor for complex layouts.

**Weaknesses for Morocco**: **billed monthly in dollars** — a recurring cost in foreign currency, sensitive to exchange rate swings. A **much smaller** ecosystem of Moroccan developers than WordPress — harder to find quick local help. Less suited to complex e-commerce with cash on delivery, an essential method in the Moroccan market. Harder to migrate later if you want to switch platforms — you don't "own" your site in the same way.

## Direct comparison

| Criteria | WordPress | Webflow |
|---|---|---|
| Starting cost | More affordable | Higher (monthly subscription) |
| Ongoing cost | Local hosting, in MAD | Subscription in USD |
| Moroccan e-commerce fit (COD) | Very good (WooCommerce) | Limited |
| Local Moroccan support | Wide | Limited |
| Content management autonomy | Good, with a bit of training | Very good, intuitive visual editor |
| Technical maintenance | On you (or a provider) | Handled by Webflow |
| SEO potential | Excellent when well configured | Good, but fewer SEO extensions |
| Full site ownership | Yes | Partial (platform dependency) |

## Which should you choose for which project?

**Choose WordPress if**: you're a **Moroccan SME, merchant, or freelancer** with a controlled budget; you plan to **sell online** with cash on delivery; you want to be able to **grow your site** easily over time; you want **local, French-speaking technical support** that's responsive.

**Choose Webflow if**: you're an **agency, international brand, or creative studio** with a comfortable budget in strong currency; **visual design is your absolute priority**, more than functional flexibility; you don't need complex e-commerce with local payment methods.

## Our recommendation for the Moroccan market

For the vast majority of Moroccan businesses — merchants, SMEs, freelancers, professionals — **WordPress remains the most relevant choice** in 2026: controlled cost in dirhams, mature local ecosystem, full compatibility with cash on delivery, and scalability that grows with your business without needing to rebuild everything.

That said, the real key isn't just the platform choice — it's the quality of execution. A poorly built WordPress site will be slower and less secure than a well-built Webflow site, and vice versa. What matters is working with a provider who truly masters the tool they've chosen, whichever it is.
